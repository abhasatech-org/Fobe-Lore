A post-mortuary analysis of Fobe As many of you know already, Fobe was breached and ultimately shut down on December 30, 2022. This announcement should hopefully clarify what led to it's death. Fobe was subject to an XSS vulnerability on our asset cdn (acdn.idk16.com) leading up to the breach of my account. The attackers had been exploiting for the weeks leading up to the breach and printing out a fake lua script in the console. This output was something along the lines of require(1234) with the ID being a place ID. This confused the hell out of me and I decided I would download the place to see what exactly it was. This was the fatal flaw that would allow the attackers into my account. The place file had been specially crafted to include javascript that would execute on my browser and send my session cookie off to a third-party domain. From there, the attackers were able to replace their own cookie with mine and gain complete access to my account, regardless of 2FA or my password. Unfortunately, the security just wasn't there on our end to prevent something like this from happening. The idea of Fobe being a complete and total shitpost led to things being rushed and minor security features like logging someone out when their region changes not being thoroughly thought out. These attackers had full access to our moderation and administration tools, meaning they could ban people at will and run scripts on any server they wanted to. Here's what we did right: - We attempted a feature parity with 2016 Roblox. - We were able to backport major Roblox client updates such as MeshParts and some of Roblox's modern Lua API. - Advertising through no means other than the community. - Modern place file support. - No paywall. Here's what we did wrong: - No security audits were performed before publishing Fobe. - Publicized/involved ourselves with ORC drama. - Lack of useful moderation tools (ie viewing alts). Goodbye, That is all. We are still no longer Fobe. For good.
